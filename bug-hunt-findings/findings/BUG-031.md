# BUG-031: mcp:command ack channel still uses predictable Date.now()+Math.random() — the one reply channel left out of the BUG-022/BUG-038 CSPRNG hardening sweep

- **Severity:** Low
- **Category:** security
- **Status:** CONFIRMED
- **Files touched:** `src/main/mcpCommand.ts`, `src/main/mcpOrchestrator.ts`
- **Roadmap:** none
- **Assigned:** _(left blank for the dev team)_

## Summary
sendMcpCommand builds the per-command reply channel as `mcp:command:ack:${Date.now()}:${Math.random().toString(36).slice(2)}` (line 57) — the same predictable-channel class fixed in flushRenderer (BUG-038, CSPRNG) and in mcpConfirm (BUG-022, randomUUID), but this channel was left on the predictable construction. Combined with the bus.once consumption flaw (BUG-030), a foreign frame that guessed/raced this channel could deliver an ignored-but-listener-consuming event, suppressing the real ack → the spawn/close path sees a "timeout" ack and the orchestrator drops its `tracked` reservation while the renderer actually created the board, leaving an untracked board outside the cap budget and the reaper.

## Where
`src/main/mcpCommand.ts:57` — `sendMcpCommand()` (the `replyChannel` construction)

## How it triggers
A foreign IPC emitter able to reach ipcMain and guess the reply channel name (requires a non-main-frame that can emit IPC).

## Verification evidence
Confirmed by direct comparison. mcpCommand.ts:57: replyChannel = `mcp:command:ack:${Date.now()}:${Math.random().toString(36).slice(2)}` — exactly the construction the sibling channels were hardened away from: mcpConfirm.ts:74 uses randomUUID with a BUG-022 comment calling Date.now()+Math.random 'both predictable', and flushChannel.ts:17-29 (BUG-038) uses randomUUID with a comment explicitly contrasting it against 'Date.now()/Math.random() (~48 bits, predictable)' and citing consistency with the BUG-022 fix. The claimed consequence chain is real in code: a foreign event consuming the once-listener (see BUG-030) yields a {ok:false,error:'timeout'} ack; spawnBoard's catch then runs `tracked.delete(id)` (mcpOrchestrator.ts:337-339) even though the renderer may have already applied addBoard — leaving a live board outside the cap budget and the reaper (reconcile only prunes `tracked`, never adopts mirror boards). Severity Low: the frame-guard blocks forged-ack content, and no foreign IPC emitter exists today (preview views have no preload, preview.ts:424), so this is the inert/unfinished half of the channel-hardening sweep.

## Suggested fix direction
Replace with `mcp:command:ack:${randomUUID()}` to match mcpConfirm/flushChannel.
