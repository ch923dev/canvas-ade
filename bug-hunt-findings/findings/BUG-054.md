# BUG-054: launch()'s spawnTerminal .then/.catch lack respawn()'s disposed/term-identity guard — a late spawn failure marks the successor session 'spawn-failed' and writes into a disposed xterm

- **Severity:** Low
- **Category:** concurrency
- **Status:** CONFIRMED
- **Files touched:** `src/renderer/src/canvas/boards/TerminalBoard.tsx`
- **Roadmap:** none
- **Assigned:** _(left blank for the dev team)_

## Summary
respawn() (line 299-310) guards both its .then and .catch with `if (termRef.current !== term) return` (the #16 fix). The initial-mount launch() closure (lines 460-478) has no such guard: if the spawn effect is torn down and re-run (config 'Apply & restart', which changes a spawn dep) while the first pty:spawn invoke is still in flight, a rejected/failed old spawn resolves after cleanup and (a) calls setState('spawn-failed') on the component — transiently mislabeling the brand-new session that is actually spawning/running — and (b) calls term.write() on the disposed old Terminal (xterm's write has no disposed guard; verified `_writeBuffer.write` is called unconditionally in the bundled lib).

## Where
`src/renderer/src/canvas/boards/TerminalBoard.tsx:469` — `launch` (closure inside `TerminalBoard`'s spawn effect; the `spawnTerminal(...).then` handler)

## How it triggers
Create a terminal with a config that makes pty:spawn fail slowly or reject (e.g. bad cwd), then hit 'Apply & restart' (or otherwise change a spawn dep) within the spawn IPC round-trip window. The stale rejection lands after the new effect ran.

## Verification evidence
Confirmed asymmetry in TerminalBoard.tsx. respawn()'s handlers both guard with `if (termRef.current !== term) return` (lines 300 and 307, the #16 fix per the comment at 279-283). launch() inside the spawn callback (455-479) performs the identical window.api.spawnTerminal invoke with identical handlers (469-478) but NO guard — neither termRef identity nor the `disposed` flag that exists in the same closure (declared 451, consulted only by adoptTerminal's .then at 496). The spawn effect's deps include board.shell/board.cwd/board.launchCommand (550-560), and TerminalConfig's 'Apply & restart' patches exactly those (TerminalConfig.tsx:65-76; its header comment says the patch 're-runs TerminalBoard's spawn effect'). So: launch()'s invoke in flight (initial spawn after first good fit, or a Start-button launch via startLaunchRef 489-494) + an Apply within the IPC round trip → cleanup runs (524-549: term.dispose() at 545, termRef nulled 546), the new effect spawns and sets 'spawning', then the stale promise settles: a spawn-failed/rejected result calls setState('spawn-failed') — mislabeling the brand-new session — and term.write() on the disposed old Terminal (470-477). Narrow window (one pty:spawn round trip) and only on a failing/rejecting spawn → Low.

## Suggested fix direction
Mirror respawn's `if (termRef.current !== term) return` (or check `disposed`) at the top of launch's .then/.catch.
